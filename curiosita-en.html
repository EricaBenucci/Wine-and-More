1. How long can a bottle of wine be stored in a cellar?
The aging potential varies depending on the wine and storage conditions. Some wines are best young, others evolve for decades.
2. What is the difference between DOC and DOCG?
DOC means Controlled Designation of Origin. DOCG adds "Guaranteed" and has stricter rules. They’re not the only designations.
3. Why swirl wine in the glass before smelling it?
It increases oxygenation, releasing aromas. It’s one of many useful techniques to fully appreciate wine.
4. What does "Riserva" mean on a bottle?
It means longer aging than the regular version. You may also see "Superiore" and other terms with specific meanings.
5. What does "elegance" mean in Grappa?
It refers to the finesse of aromas and harmony of flavors—key elements during tasting.
6. Why are mountain and island wines so special?
Altitude gives freshness and acidity; the sea brings minerality and aroma. These are just some unique features.
7. Easy rule for food & wine pairing?
Red wine with meat, white with fish: a basic rule to start, but there’s much more to explore and enjoy!
8. How important is a wine's vintage?
Extremely! Weather conditions affect the grapes and therefore the quality of the wine from year to year.
9. How do you recognize quality EVOO?
Fresh, intense aroma is a key sign. Smell, taste, and check certified label info for quality assurance.
10. What grapes are used in Chianti?
Sangiovese is the star, but other red varieties may be blended in to add specific characteristics.
11. Is Grappa served cold or room temperature?
Young, aromatic Grappa is good chilled. Aged Grappa is better at room temperature to express aromas.
12. What are "Super Tuscans"?
High-quality Tuscan reds made outside DOC/G rules, often blending Sangiovese with Cabernet, Merlot or Syrah.
