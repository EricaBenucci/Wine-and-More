1. How long can a bottle of wine be stored in a cellar?
The aging potential varies depending on the type of wine and the storage conditions. Some wines are meant to be drunk young, while others can age for decades.
2. What is the difference between DOC and DOCG?
DOC indicates Controlled Designation of Origin, DOCG adds a guarantee and stricter regulations. They're not the only designations.
3. Why swirl wine in the glass before smelling it?
Swirling increases oxygenation, releasing more aromas. It's a helpful technique to enhance wine appreciation.
4. What does "Riserva" mean on a wine label?
It usually means longer aging. Labels might also show other terms like "Superiore" with different meanings.
5. What does "elegance" mean in Grappa?
It refers to the finesse and harmony of aromas—qualities observed during tasting.
6. Why are mountain and island wines so special?
Altitude brings freshness, the sea adds sapidity and aroma. Just two of many factors that make them unique.
7. Food and wine pairing: is there an easy rule?
Red with meat, white with fish is a starting point, but exploring deeper combinations is more fun!
8. How important is a wine's vintage?
Very! Weather affects the grapes and the wine quality from that year.
9. EVOO: how to recognize quality?
Fresh, intense aroma is a good sign. Smell and taste are key. Labels also help ensure certified quality.
10. Chianti: what are its main grape varieties?
Sangiovese is the main one, but others can be blended in to add distinct characteristics.
11. Grappa: cold or room temperature?
Young aromatic ones are nice chilled. Aged Grappa shows better at room temperature.
12. What is meant by "Super Tuscans"?
High-quality Tuscan reds made outside DOC/DOCG rules, often blending international grapes with Sangiovese.
